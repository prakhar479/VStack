How ChunkPaxos Works

Phase 1: Prepare

The uploader sends a PREPARE request with a ballot number to all storage nodes. Nodes respond with a PROMISE if they haven't seen a higher ballot number. This phase ensures no conflicting writes are in progress.

Phase 2: Accept

Once a quorum (majority) of nodes promise, the uploader sends ACCEPT requests with the actual chunk data. Nodes write the chunk to disk and respond with ACK. The uploader waits for a quorum of ACKs before committing.

Phase 3: Commit

After receiving a quorum of ACKs, the uploader notifies the metadata service to commit the chunk placement. The chunk is now officially stored and available for download by clients.

Benefits of ChunkPaxos

  • Parallel Execution: Different chunks can be written simultaneously without conflicts
  • Reduced Messages: 6 messages vs 9 for standard Paxos (33% reduction)
  • Simplified Logic: Exploits domain knowledge that chunk writes are independent
  • Fault Tolerance: Continues operating with minority node failures
  • Consistency: Guarantees strong consistency for chunk placement
  • Conflict Resolution: Handles concurrent uploaders with ballot numbers
